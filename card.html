Amrita sher-Gil

Known as one of India’s pioneering modernists, Sher-Gil’s work merged Western styles with Indian subjects. Inspired by European modernism, she painted striking portraits and scenes of Indian life, highlighting women’s experiences and everyday rural landscapes.

Tibyet Mehta

A key figure in Indian modernism, Mehta’s work is known for its powerful, angular forms and use of intense color. His paintings often explored themes of human struggle and violence, with iconic series like Mahishasura reflecting mythology and contemporary angst.

Rabindranath Tagore

Though primarily known as a poet and philosopher, Tagore’s paintings showcased a distinct style marked by surreal, expressive forms. His art reflected his imagination and deep connection to nature, which he also expressed in his literary work.

Jamini Roy

Roy’s art celebrated Indian folk traditions. Inspired by Bengali patua art, his work focused on simplicity, using bold colors and traditional motifs that resonated with Indian culture, portraying rural life, mythology, and tribal influences.

Satish gujral

An artist, architect, and sculptor, Gujral’s work draws on his traumatic experiences of partition, often focusing on human suffering and resilience. His versatile creations in painting, sculpture, and architecture reflect a blend of Indian traditions with modern influences.

Raja Ravi Verma

Often called the "Father of Modern Indian Art," Varma is famous for blending European realism with Indian themes. His paintings of mythological figures made gods and goddesses accessible, popularizing their imagery among the masses.
